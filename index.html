Which One’s Got the Lowest Inflation Rate?

Bitcoin

Inflation Rate: 0.84%

Thanks to the “halving” event in 2024, Bitcoin's supply is strictly limited, with a maximum of 21 million BTC.

Gold

Inflation Rate: 1.5% - 2%

The annual increase in the supply of gold is around 1.5% to 2%, with about 3,200 tonnes mined per year.

Fiat Currency

Inflation Rate: 3% - 10% (or higher)

Inflation in fiat currencies depends on central bank policies, often reaching high and unpredictable levels.

In summary, Bitcoin has the lowest inflation rate, followed by gold, while fiat currency has the highest and most unpredictable rates.

This is a Crypto Debit Card Built by Lemon, an Argentinian Startup

What's the Point?

Argentinians struggle with currency depreciation. In other words, the currency they are paid with tends to crash in value, eroding their buying power.

Lemon thinks stablecoins, cryptocurrencies pegged to a fiat currency such as the US dollar, can help.

Explanation from Lemon's CFO, Maxi Raimondi

"It’s like any prepaid Visa card that you can use at any physical or digital store around the world that accepts Visa.

The difference is that your money is stored in your Lemon account, in the cryptocurrency you choose, such as USDC (a stablecoin pegged to the US dollar). When you buy something at your local bakery, we convert your USDC to local peso using a real-time exchange rate.

The merchant is paid in peso, but your money has been sitting in USDC until the very last moment. It thus hasn’t suffered any drastic drop in worth.

You might ask what exchange rate we use. That’s a good question, since Argentina is known for having over 20 exchange rates.

Lemon uses the Blue Chip Swap rate, which is calculated through a byzantine mechanism. In short: Argentinians buy US bonds in pesos and resell those bonds in dollars. An implied exchange rate is extrapolated.

While imperfect, we find this rate to be more accurate than both the government exchange rate and black market exchange rates."
